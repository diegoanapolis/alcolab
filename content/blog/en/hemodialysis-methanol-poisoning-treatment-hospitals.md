# Hemodialysis: How Hospitals Treat Methanol Poisoning

**Hemodialysis for methanol poisoning** represents one of the most critical challenges in global emergency medicine. When someone ingests methanol, time is gold. The first minutes and hours determine whether the patient will survive without permanent sequelae.

AlcoLab offers an innovative preventive solution at [alcolab.org](https://alcolab.org), allowing identification of potentially contaminated beverages **before consumption.** But for those already exposed, advanced hospital treatment is essential.

## How Methanol Poisons the Body

The danger of methanol is not in the alcohol itself, but in its metabolism. When ingested, methanol is converted by the liver into formaldehyde and then into **formic acid**—an extremely toxic substance.

Formic acid damages:

- **Optic nerve** → causing blindness
- **Central nervous system** → causing neurological damage
- **Vital organs** → causing severe metabolic acidosis

This chemical reaction occurs primarily in the first 12 to 24 hours after consumption. **For this reason**, rapid diagnosis is essential.

The person may initially appear merely intoxicated by conventional alcohol. **However**, they are developing a silent medical emergency.

## Early Diagnosis: The First Critical Step

Modern hospitals use several markers to identify **methanol poisoning.**

The **gas chromatography** test is the gold standard, directly measuring blood methanol levels.

### Diagnostic Markers

Beyond this, doctors analyze:

- **Elevated osmol gap** (difference between measured and calculated osmolality)
- **Anion gap metabolic acidosis** (acidosis with a large ion difference)
- **Arterial blood gas** (low pH and bicarbonate)
- **Elevated lactate** (indicator of severity)

These combined findings allow confirmation of diagnosis even when gas chromatography is not immediately available.

**In this sense**, every minute counts to start treatment.

## Three-Pillar Treatment Protocol

International hospitals follow a well-established protocol that combines three fundamental pillars.

### Pillar 1: Pharmaceutical Antidote – Fomepizole

[Fomepizole antidote for methanol](/blog/en/fomepizole-antidote-methanol-poisoning-how-it-works.md) (4-methylpyrazole) is the drug of choice in high-complexity medical centers.

It works by blocking the enzyme alcohol dehydrogenase. **Thus**, it prevents methanol from being converted into formaldehyde and formic acid—its toxic forms.

#### Dosage Protocol

Dosage follows strictly:

| Phase | Dose | Frequency |
|------|------|-----------|
| Loading | 15 mg/kg IV | Once |
| Maintenance (first 48h) | 10 mg/kg | Every 12 hours |
| Maintenance (after 48h) | 15 mg/kg | Every 12 hours |

**Fomepizole antidote** is extremely effective, drastically reducing mortality when applied early. **However**, in many developing countries, the medication is unavailable or prohibitively expensive.

### Pillar 1 Alternative: Intravenous Ethanol

When **fomepizole antidote for methanol** is unavailable, IV ethanol is a proven alternative. It works through the same mechanism—blocking methanol metabolism—but requires rigorous monitoring in an intensive care unit.

#### IV Ethanol Protocol

The protocol is complex:

- **Loading dose:** 10 ml/kg of 10% solution IV over 30-40 minutes
- **Maintenance:** 10% of loading dose every hour
- **Goal:** Maintain serum ethanol between 100-150 mg/dL
- **Monitoring:** Continuous glucose monitoring (hypoglycemia is a critical risk)

The disadvantage is that IV ethanol causes inebriation, respiratory depression, and additional risks.

**Still**, it's better than allowing methanol to be metabolized.

### Pillar 2: Hemodialysis – Direct Toxin Removal

**Hemodialysis for methanol poisoning** is indicated when:

- Blood methanol level **> 50 mg/dL**
- Severe metabolic acidosis **(pH < 7.1)**
- Any evidence of **visual injury**
- **Kidney failure**
- Any poisoning in **children**

Unlike other situations, **hemodialysis for methanol poisoning** is rapidly effective. **After all**, it removes both methanol and formic acid directly from circulation.

#### How Hemodialysis Works

The procedure:

1. **Removes methanol** directly through the dialysis membrane
2. **Removes accumulated formic acid** (responsible for blindness)
3. **Corrects metabolic acidosis** simultaneously
4. **Patients improve visibly** after 4-6 hours of dialysis

The global limitation is access: not all hospitals have hemodialysis available. In some countries, there are only a few machines for millions of people.

### Pillar 3: Vital Support and Metabolism

Treatment does not end with **fomepizole antidote** and dialysis. Hospitals add multiple complementary measures.

#### IV Sodium Bicarbonate

Corrects metabolic acidosis, raising blood pH to safe values.

**Dosage:** Adjusted according to arterial blood gas.

#### Folinic Acid (Leucovorin)

Serves as an essential cofactor. Formic acid is converted to CO₂ and water only when folinic acid is available.

**Doses:** 1 mg/kg every 4-6 hours.

#### Oxygen and Ventilatory Support

Maintains adequate saturation. Acidosis affects breathing. **Continuous monitoring** of SpO2 is essential.

#### Electrolytes and Glucose

Continuous monitoring, as secondary complications frequently arise during treatment.

## Prognosis: Time Decides

Survival depends on critical factors:

| Factor | Importance | Observation |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Time to treatment** | CRITICAL | First hour vs 12th hour = huge difference |
| **Amount ingested** | Critical | Smaller doses = better prognosis |
| **pH at admission** | Critical | The more acidic, the worse the prognosis |
| **Glasgow Coma Scale** | Important | Reflects brain damage |
| **Lactate level** | Important | Predictor of mortality |
| **Age and comorbidities** | Important | Influences recovery |

### Mortality Rates

Mortality varies between:

- **Without treatment:** 50-60%
- **With ethanol and hemodialysis:** 20-40%
- **With fomepizole and hemodialysis:** 5-10%

**First**, access to **fomepizole antidote** drastically reduces mortality. **Furthermore**, the earlier treatment begins, the better the prognosis.

## The Global Challenge: Lack of Fomepizole and Unequal Access

The international reality is concerning. Countries with economic sanctions (such as Iran) face chronic shortages of **fomepizole antidote for methanol.**

India experienced outbreaks with limited access. Most African nations do not have sufficient **hemodialysis.**

This creates brutal disparity:

- **Zurich:** Poisoned patient will survive
- **Lagos:** Risks are exponentially higher

**In this sense**, geographic location determines clinical outcome.
