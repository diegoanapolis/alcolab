## What is methanol: a silent threat in beverages

**What is methanol?** It's a simple chemical substance, yet extremely toxic to the human body. Its chemical formula is CH₃OH, which makes it the simplest alcohol that exists in nature. First and foremost, it's often called "wood alcohol" because historically it was produced by the dry distillation of wood. Today, production is done synthetically at large scale, making it widely available and, unfortunately, accessible to beverage adulterators.

The core problem is serious: methanol is practically indistinguishable from ethanol. Both are colorless liquids. Both have similar odor. Both have practically identical taste. For this reason, it's impossible to identify the presence of methanol in a beverage using the senses alone.

Color reveals nothing. Aroma reveals nothing. Taste reveals nothing. This imperceptibility makes adulterations dangerous and invisible to the unsuspecting consumer.

## Methanol vs. Ethanol: the fundamental differences

The difference between **methanol and ethanol** is small in molecular terms, but gigantic in toxicity. As demonstrated in the table below, even basic chemical properties differ significantly:

| Property | Methanol | Ethanol |
|---|---|---|
| **Chemical Formula** | CH₃OH | C₂H₅OH |
| **Density (g/mL)** | 0.791 | 0.789 |
| **Viscosity (mPa·s)** | 0.544 | 1.200 |
| **Boiling Point (°C)** | 64.7 | 78.4 |
| **Molecular Weight (g/mol)** | 32 | 46 |
| **Toxicity** | Lethal | Safe (moderate) |

The ethanol found in legitimate alcoholic beverages has the formula C₂H₅OH. Methanol is CH₃OH—one fewer carbon and one more hydrogen. This minimal change causes devastating consequences.

## The toxic pathway: lethal metabolism

While ethanol is metabolized in a controlled manner into acetaldehyde and then acetic acid, methanol follows a completely different pathway. In this sense, understanding this biochemical distinction is fundamental to comprehending why a similar substance causes so much damage.

**Ethanol Metabolism (safe):**
1. Ethanol → Acetaldehyde (enzyme: alcohol dehydrogenase)
2. Acetaldehyde → Acetic acid (enzyme: aldehyde dehydrogenase)
3. Result: Acetic acid is harmless and easily eliminated

**Methanol Metabolism (toxic):**
1. Methanol → Formaldehyde (enzyme: alcohol dehydrogenase)
2. Formaldehyde → Formic acid (enzyme: aldehyde dehydrogenase)
3. Result: Formic acid is highly toxic

When you ingest methanol, your body begins an extremely harmful metabolization process. First and foremost, an enzyme called alcohol dehydrogenase converts methanol into formaldehyde—the same substance used to preserve cadavers in laboratories. This stage is already dangerous.

Subsequently, the formaldehyde is converted into formic acid (or methanoic acid), where the true danger resides. Formic acid is a very strong acid. It attacks vital tissues in the body. Particularly, it has special affinity for the optic nerve—responsible for vision.

## Natural methanol: safe concentrations

It's true that methanol appears naturally in small quantities in fermented beverages. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the natural concentration of methanol in alcoholic beverages is below 0.1%. Therefore, in such reduced quantities, it poses no health risk.

Furthermore, Brazilian legislation (MAPA Normative Instruction 13/2005) permits up to 200 mg of methanol per 100 mL of anhydrous alcohol in distilled beverages. These levels are completely safe when they arise naturally from the fermentation process.

The danger emerges when criminals deliberately add methanol to beverages. The concentrations reach levels between 20% and 80%—quantities a hundred times greater than the natural ones.

## Consequences of methanol poisoning

The most characteristic consequence of methanol intoxication is blindness. Formic acid destroys retinal cells. It causes inflammation of the optic nerve. It frequently leads to permanent loss of vision—irreversible damage even with appropriate medical treatment.

Furthermore, formic acid causes severe metabolic acidosis. This is a dangerous imbalance in blood pH. It can lead to systemic collapse. After all, practically no organ remains exempt from the toxic effects.

The damages include:
- Permanent blindness (most common consequence if not fatal)
- Severe metabolic acidosis
- Acute kidney failure
- Pancreas inflammation (pancreatitis)
- Cerebral edema
- Cardiovascular collapse

## How much methanol is needed to cause damage?

The lethal doses of methanol are frighteningly small. The lethal dose generally varies between 1 to 2 mL per kilogram of body weight. For a person weighing 80 kg, this means that approximately 80 mL of pure methanol could be fatal.

However, the reality is even more concerning:
- **10 mL of pure methanol** = Irreversible blindness
- **20-30 mL of pure methanol** = Potentially fatal
- **80 mL (for 80 kg)** = Estimated lethal dose

It's worth noting that individual sensitivity varies. Factors such as age, general health, liver capacity, and previous food consumption influence absorption and metabolism. However, none of these factors make methanol safe. Only a few offer minimal and temporary protection.

## Adulterated beverages: dangerous concentrations

A concrete example illustrates the danger. Consider a beverage containing 20% methanol in its total alcoholic composition. A 50 mL glass of this beverage would contain 10 mL of pure methanol—exactly the dose that causes blindness.

A 700 mL bottle of distilled spirits with 20% methanol substitution would contain approximately 56 mL of pure methanol. This is potentially fatal.

Studies of historical outbreaks show the reality:
- Salvador outbreak 1999: concentrations between 2.85% and 24.84%
- Brazil outbreak 2025: concentrations of 20% or greater

A simple contaminated glass can result in tragedy.

## Why is methanol added to beverages?

The answer is simple and deplorable: profit. Methanol is significantly cheaper than ethanol in many countries, including Brazil. Criminals add methanol to beverages to increase the apparent alcohol content. They sell an inferior quality product at the price of a legitimate beverage.

This practice is particularly common in:
- Clandestine distilleries
- Counterfeit beverages
- Dishonest establishments
- Informal street sales

The criminal saves on production costs. The consumer pays full price. The consumer potentially pays with their health or their life.

## Industrial uses of methanol

Outside the context of adulterated beverages, methanol has many legitimate uses:

- **Industrial solvent** — in various chemical processes
- **Alternative fuel** — in specific applications
- **Formaldehyde precursor** — for chemical synthesis
- **Cleaning liquid** — in antifreeze products for car windows
- **Organic synthesis** — in various chemical processes

This widespread industrial availability makes it easier for criminals to obtain methanol in large quantities. The combination of ease of access, low price, and profit potential creates a perfect environment for criminal activity.

Meanwhile, unsuspecting consumers are left completely without ways to protect themselves—until now.

## How to protect yourself: the importance of screening

Faced with this invisible threat, the inevitable question is: how can I know if a beverage contains methanol? Unfortunately, traditional and homemade methods are ineffective. You cannot rely on your senses. Color is deceptive. Aroma is deceptive. Taste is deceptive.

Therefore, you cannot simply avoid beverages of unknown origin. Sophisticated counterfeits can perfectly imitate famous brands.

**The best strategy is preventive screening before consumption.** Furthermore, it's crucial to purchase beverages only from reliable and established establishments. When possible, buy sealed beverages. Prefer authentic products from certified brands.

For those who want to go further, screening technologies are available. [AlcoLab](https://alcolab.org) is a free and open-source web application that can help detect the presence of methanol in alcoholic beverages. Using only a syringe, a kitchen scale, and a smartphone, it's possible to perform a simple test that provides reliable results.

This tool represents a significant advance in the ability to protect yourself against adulterated beverages. In this sense, there's no valid excuse to remain unprotected.

## Conclusion: knowledge and prevention save lives

Understanding what methanol is and why it's dangerous is the first step to protecting yourself. Methanol is a lethal chemical substance that causes irreversible damage to the human body. Especially to the eyes and nervous system, it causes devastating effects.

Its similarity to ethanol makes detection impossible without appropriate equipment. But this doesn't mean you're defenseless. Accordingly, the combination of conscious shopping, knowledge about the problem, and access to screening tools like AlcoLab creates an effective barrier against this threat.

In a world where adulterated beverages are a reality, information and prevention are your best allies.

**Remember: when it comes to what methanol is in beverages, prevention is not just recommended—it's essential for your survival.**
