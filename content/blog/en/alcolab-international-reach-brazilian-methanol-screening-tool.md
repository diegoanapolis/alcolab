## Why the World Needs Methanol Screening

### Global Scale of Poisoning

The British Foreign Office (FCDO) documented methanol poisoning in **29 countries**. Médecins Sans Frontières recorded cases in **185 countries**.

In sum, the numbers are alarming:
- **40,100 people** affected since 1998
- **14,323 confirmed deaths**
- Each death could have been prevented with early detection

Therefore, screening tools like AlcoLab are essential.

### Epidemiological Alert for the Americas

In October 2025, PAHO/WHO issued alert covering **5 countries in the Americas**. The Brazil outbreak confirmed **200+ methanol poisoning cases**.

Therefore, the situation is urgent. The need for quick screening is immediate. In this sense, AlcoLab offers practical response.

### Limited Laboratory Infrastructure in Developing Countries

Many regions face reality different from well-equipped labs. Indonesia, India, Iran, Cambodia, and Vietnam deal with chronic adulteration problems.

Furthermore, these countries lack modern laboratory infrastructure. A gas chromatograph costs over R$30,000. For local governments, it's inaccessible.

## Taxes Creating Demand for Illegal Beverages

Contamination risk doesn't arise by accident. It often emerges from perverse economic incentives.

Indonesia imposes **150% tax on legal alcoholic beverages**. This excessive taxation makes legal alcohol very expensive. Therefore, consumers seek untaxed illegal beverages.

Small local manufacturers produce rudimentarily. They frequently adulterate with methanol — cheaper than ethanol.

Therefore, AlcoLab offers consumers a chance to verify. It's probably their only real protection. After all, methanol screening before consumption saves lives.
